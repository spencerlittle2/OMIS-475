Door County's wildflowers during the Summer months

Summer season in Wisconsin's Door County is a must visit for a Anthophile. During Summer months, Door County is popular for the Yellow Lady's Slipper flower.

    Characteristics of Yellow Lady's Slipper flower:
  • 4 — 24 inches tall
  • Perennial
  • Native Plant
  • Grows in wet, shaded deciduous woods, swamps and bogs
  • Considered an orchid
  • Fruit is a single red berry
  • Offical flower of Door County
