2.     Compound Query with a Join Statement- CUSTOMERS and PARTS Tables

 

 

 

SELECT         LastName, FirstName, EmailAddress, PhoneNumber, Description, Warranty

FROM             CUSTOMERS

JOIN               Parts

ON                  customers.CustomerID = parts.PartID

WHERE          DESCRIPTION like   ‘Performance Battery’

AND               Warranty NOT LIKE  ‘LifeTime’;

 

§  The management of JLT was just informed by their supplier of Performance Battery’s that a few customers with performance battery’s around the nation have experienced battery shortages and a recall was implemented for all units sold.  In my query I decided to use “NOT LIKE ‘LifeTime’’ for warranty because the customers with a lifetime warranty would not have to worry about any issues since they have a lifetime warranty.  The customers who do not have a lifetime warranty will most likely be wondering if this is covered and management at JLT Performance will be informing these customers via email.

 

 

 

 

3.     Compound Query with a Join Statement- BRANCH and MECHANIC Tables

 

 

 

 

 

 

SELECT                     BranchCity, BranchState, Zip, MechanicFN, MechanicLN, Experience

FROM                         branch

JOIN                           mechanic

ON                              branch.BranchID = mechanic.BranchID

WHERE                      BranchState = ‘IL’;

 

 

§  The management at JLT Performance is considering to hire more mechanics for the State of Illinois.  JLT Performance would like to add more mechanics because Illinois only has two active mechanics.  With this query management is able to search any state to determine if more mechanics are needed.

5.     Compound Query with a Join Statement- PARTS and LABOR Tables

 

 

SELECT         Description, Warranty, JobType, ServiceID

FROM             parts

JOIN               labor

ON                  parts.PartID = labor.LaborID

WHERE          JobType = ‘Engine Performance’

 

 

 

§  The management of JLT Performance was curious on how certain services were performing.  Management decided to query a result to see how engine performance was doing and found out that only two services for engine performance has been performed.  Management has come to conclusion that Engine Performance services are underperforming.  A manager can use this query for all of the different service types individually.  This can be used to decide on whether to decide to stop or expand specific services.

 

 

 

 

 

6.     Compound Query with a Join Statement- MECHANIC and YEARLYSALARY Tables

 

 

 

SELECT         MechanicFN, MechanicLN, Experience, NewbieMechanic

FROM             mechanic

JOIN               yearlysalary

ON                  mechanic.MechanicID = yearlysalary.MechanicID

WHERE          Experience = ‘Newbie Mechanic’

ORDER BY    MechanicFN;

 

§  The management of JLT Performance is curious on how many Newbie mechanics they have working and are trying to determine if they should promote these mechanics.  Management is also curious on whether they will be have enough Newbie Mechanics if they decided to promote any.  A query was written that displays all Newbie mechanics the query shows 5 Newbie Mechanics across the company.  If management decided to promote any of the Newbie Mechanics they will face a shortage of Newbie mechanics and be required to pay a higher salary.  The query was ordered by Mechanic First Name to make it easier and more organized to read.

 

 

7.     Compound Query with a Join Statement- PARTS and PARTSUSED Tables

 

 

SELECT         Description, SalePrice, Price, QuanityUsed

FROM             parts

JOIN               partused

ON                  parts.PartID = partsused.PartsUsedID

WHERE          QuanityUsed   > ‘3’

ORDER BY    Description;

 

 

§  The management of JLT Performance wants to create a minimum stock level in order to keep a certain amount of parts in inventory.  JLT is wondering if a quantity of 3 in inventory would a safe number to have.  After management ran this query they became aware that 8 service jobs have used over 3 quantity at a single time.  Management determined that 3 quantity of inventory is not a safe number.  The query is ordered by description making it more clear and easier to read.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

8.     Compound Query with a Join Statement- BRANCH and SERVICE

 

 

 

SELECT         BranchCity, BranchState, Zip, ServiceDateStarted, ServiceDateEnd

FROM             branch

JOIN               service

ON                  branch.BranchID = service.BranchID

WHERE          ServiceDateStarted BETWEEN ‘2017-01-01’ and ‘2018-01-01’

ORDER BY    BranchCity;

 

§  The management of JLT Performance is trying to determine their performance for 2017.  JLT Performance decided that the best way to determine their performance is to see all of the total services from 2017.  This query allows JLT Performance to see the City, State, and Zip for all services performed in 2017.  This query shows 19 services for the year of 2017.
